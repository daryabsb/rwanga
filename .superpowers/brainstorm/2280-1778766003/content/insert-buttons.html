Inserting things — the "+" affordances

This editor has two grammars: structured screenplay (scenes) and free rich text (paragraphs, headings, notes). You described two round "+" buttons on opposite sides. Here's that idea on the actual paper page, plus two alternatives. In all three, the full block catalogue (heading, quote, list, page break, title…) is reached by typing / on an empty line — the "+" is only for the common moves.

Both circles ride the cursor's line. Left (blue) = new Scene — inserts a scene after this one, cursor lands in the Setting zone. Right (grey) = Free text — inside a scene inserts inline free-text; outside a scene inserts a paragraph.

A. Two circles, opposite margins

Exactly what you described. The two grammars get one button each, on the two page margins, both tracking the cursor line. Direct actions — no menus hang off them. / handles everything else.

B. One circle, 2-item popup

Same two actions, but one button and a minimal popup. Keeps the right margin clear, no mousing across the page — but it's one extra click and a small menu, which you've disliked before.

No "+" buttons at all. Everything — scene, free text, heading, list — comes from typing / on an empty line. Plus Ctrl+Enter for a new scene.

C. Slash only, no buttons

The cleanest page — nothing floats in the margins. But it's the least discoverable for a new writer, and you specifically asked for the visible "+" affordance.

My recommendation: A — two circles, opposite margins. It's what you designed, it maps the two grammars to two buttons cleanly, and with a real page the two margins are natural homes for them. / and Ctrl+Enter still exist for power users. One caveat to confirm: on a narrow window the right margin can get tight — I'd have the right "+" tuck just inside the page edge rather than off in the desk. Pick one.
