Where does "SCENE 1" live?

You said the bare number isn't enough — the word SCENE matters "for the mentality." There are three real places it can go. Each card shows the same two scenes on the dark paper. The heading treatment shown is C (gray band + underline) — your default — but remember all three treatments stay configurable.

INT. CAFÉ - NIGHT SCENE 1
A dimly lit café. Rain on the windows.
SARAH
You're late.
EXT. STREET - NIGHT SCENE 2
Wet asphalt mirrors the streetlights.

A. Margin label

"SCENE 1" sits in the left margin beside the heading — like a printed shooting script, but with the word spelled out. The heading line stays clean: just the slug. Doesn't print into the page body.

SCENE 1
INT. CAFÉ - NIGHT
A dimly lit café. Rain on the windows.
SARAH
You're late.
SCENE 2
EXT. STREET - NIGHT
Wet asphalt mirrors the streetlights.

B. Two-line heading

"SCENE 1" is its own small line inside the heading band, above the slug. This matches your earlier "put it on the next line" note. The whole block reads as one unmistakable header unit.

SCENE 1  |  INT. CAFÉ - NIGHT
A dimly lit café. Rain on the windows.
SARAH
You're late.
SCENE 2  |  EXT. STREET - NIGHT
Wet asphalt mirrors the streetlights.

C. Inline prefix

"SCENE 1" is the first part of the heading line itself: SCENE 1 | INT. CAFÉ - NIGHT. One line, everything visible, but the slug now shares the line with the number.

My recommendation: B — the two-line heading. It directly answers both things you've asked for: the word SCENE is right there "for the mentality," and it's literally "on the next line" from the INT/EXT slug. The header becomes one solid block the eye can't miss when scrolling. A is the most print-traditional; C gets cramped once locations get long. Your call — and tell me if numbering should auto-renumber when scenes are reordered (I'm assuming yes).
