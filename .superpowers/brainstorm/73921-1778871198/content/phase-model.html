Section 2 — Four-Phase Trajectory

Months are post v01-launch. Numbers are order-of-magnitude estimates to calibrate decisions, not commitments. They get refined as Phase 1 actuals come in.

Phase 1 — Zero State Months 0-3
Shipsv01 IDE (free + Pro entitlement, branded PDF / DOCX); Platform with hard signup gate, email confirm, project mgmt basics, light AI touchpoints (title suggestions, smart search) via Ollama/Gemma free + Claude Pro
Infra1 platform server (Django + DB) + 1 small VPS for Ollama + Claude API key
TeamYou, evenings/weekends
Monthly cost~$60-230 (infra $30-80, Claude $10-50, ads $20-100)
Monthly rev$0-200 (a few Pro trickle in)
MarketingPersonal Kurdish/ME film community outreach, first YouTube/Medium tutorials, $20-100 experimental IG/TikTok ads
Trigger to Phase 2: ~50-100 confirmed accounts + steady IDE downloads + no critical production bugs
Phase 2 — Adoption Months 3-9
ShipsIDE iteration on user feedback, Platform UX polish, content cadence (YouTube weekly, blog), localized landing pages (Kurdish/Arabic), maybe additional Pro perks (templates library, project archives)
InfraSame shape, slight DB/storage growth as user uploads accumulate
TeamYou, evenings/weekends; possibly a Kurdish community part-timer for translation/outreach (gig-rate)
Monthly cost~$230-750 (infra $80-200, Claude $50-150, ads $100-400)
Monthly rev$300-1500 MRR target
MarketingEmail-list activation (the asset you've been building from day 1), scaled paid ads to top-performing personas, partnership feelers with Kurdish/ME film schools
Trigger to Phase 3: MRR consistently > (infra + Claude) → profitable; ~500-1000 active accounts; email list of 1500+
Phase 3 — Monetization Maturity Months 9-18
Shipsv2 AI launch pair: cross-language collab (the headline) + script breakdown. Credit pack billing. 100MB free cloud sync tier. Platform binding (push from IDE to Project).
InfraScale platform server (more RAM or 2nd instance), DB scale, AI inference via hosted APIs (Anthropic/Google/Together) — variable cost from here on
TeamFirst hire: support + community ops (part-time → full-time as MRR allows). You still product/engineering.
Monthly cost~$800-2900 (infra $200-600, AI $300-1500, marketing $300-800)
Monthly rev$2000-6000 MRR (Pro subs + credit packs)
Marketing"Cross-language collab" demo as headline content; the Swedish↔Saudi pitch video; conference / festival presence
Trigger to Phase 4: MRR > your day-job income reliably for 2-3 months → you go full-time on Rwanga
Phase 4 — Reinvestment Scaling Months 18+
ShipsFull v2 AI rollout. v2.5 chatbot (right-inspector). More language anchors (Arabic, Persian). MCP/CLI delivery surfaces. Advanced Platform Pro features. Dataset pipeline + fine-tune prep.
InfraMulti-region or cloud-native scaling. Dedicated AI inference budget. Phase-2 fine-tune deploy via Together/Fireworks if dataset ready.
Team2-4 people: you (full-time, product+eng), ops/community hire (now full-time), maybe 1 engineer, contractor curators per language
Monthly cost~$8.5K-26K (infra $1K-3K, AI $1.5K-5K, marketing $1K-3K, team $5K-15K)
Monthly rev$15K-40K MRR target
MarketingCross-cultural collaboration as category narrative. Conference circuit. Strategic partnerships (Slemani University Cinema Dept, festivals)
Phase exit: Stable team, sustainable margins, conscious decision point: stay indie at scale, raise to accelerate, or hold steady
Three things worth pointing out

1. Phase 1 cost ceiling is low enough that your day job covers any shortfall comfortably. Even worst-case ($230/mo costs, $0 revenue), that's well under a typical month's restaurant budget. Risk is low; learning per dollar is high.

2. Phase 3 is where the v2 AI brainstorm actually executes. Don't ship v2 AI in Phase 1 or 2 — the dataset isn't there, the user base isn't there, and the cost ramp would crater unit economics. The Phase 2 free-tier accumulation IS the dataset for Phase 3's fine-tune.

3. The Phase 3→4 trigger (MRR > day job) is the most important business milestone — it converts a side project into a company. Plan the support hire (Phase 3) to happen before you go full-time, so you're never simultaneously hiring and leaving stability.
