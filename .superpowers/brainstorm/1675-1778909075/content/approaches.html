Two business-model approaches

Both respect everything we've locked (OSS IDE, network-boundary fence, evenings/weekends pace, international positioning). They differ on where the funnel begins and where Pro money first lands.

A

Trojan horse via OSS IDE Recommended

The free OSS IDE is the acquisition channel. Writers download, write offline, hit the natural wall — they want a branded PDF for their pitch, or DOCX for their editor — and that's where Pro lands. Platform exists from day 1 as "where your scripts live across machines" with soft monetization (3-script-per-Project limit). Lightweight AI touchpoints on Platform hint at what's coming in v2.

Pace

Months 0-3: launch + iterate. Months 4-9: first paying users trickle in. Months 9-15: first ops hire trigger when MRR justifies it.

Marketing

Organic-first: YouTube/Medium tutorials, Kurdish/ME film communities personally cultivated. Small targeted IG/TikTok ads to indie screenwriter personas. Total ad budget ~$50-200/mo in early phase.

Revenue source order

1. IDE Pro (branded PDF + DOCX) — individual writers
2. Platform Pro (3-script limit lift) — serious users
3. v2 AI credit packs — later, when AI ships

Why it fits

Matches every locked decision: file-sovereign IDE, free-forever OSS, fence rule, sovereignty constraint. Low capital requirement. Patient enough for evenings/weekends. Pro revenue arrives naturally as users hit walls, not as a hard sell.

Risk

Slow ramp — may take 6-9 months to see real Pro revenue. Branded-export + DOCX might be too narrow a Pro hook for IDE-only users.

B

Platform-first SaaS

Position Rwanga primarily as a web Platform. The IDE is a "downloadable companion" but the Platform is the home base. Pro features concentrate on the Platform side. Higher engagement, clearer "you need an account" UX, easier to monetize.

Pace

Months 0-3: Platform launch with IDE as companion. Months 4-6: paid signups via web onboarding funnel. Faster monetization curve than A.

Marketing

Paid ads heavier (web SaaS conversion needs visibility). Landing-page-driven funnel. SEO investment. Higher ad budget ~$300-800/mo for meaningful reach.

Revenue source order

1. Platform Pro subscription — primary
2. v2 AI on Platform — later
3. IDE branded export — niche bonus

Why someone would pick it

Faster revenue (web SaaS conversion is well-understood). Easier to talk to investors later if you ever want to. More room for upsells (team plans, agency plans). Familiar pattern.

Risk

Dilutes the IDE-is-sovereign promise. Platform is harder to differentiate against Final Draft/WriterDuet without v2 AI shipped. Higher ad spend conflicts with evenings/weekends bootstrap. The "gift to writers" positioning weakens.

My recommendation

A — Trojan horse via OSS IDE. Your runway is patient. The OSS IDE is already your goodwill engine and category differentiator. Forcing the funnel through Platform-first SaaS would burn the moat you just built. Better: let writers fall in love with the IDE first, let Pro money find you when they hit natural walls, then deploy v2 AI on top of a loyal base instead of a cold acquisition funnel.
