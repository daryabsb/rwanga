Search Results
The scene list gives way to context snippets. Three occurrences of "kitchen" across three scenes. The writer sees: where it is, what surrounds it — exactly like an IDE search or Google snippet inside the screenplay.
1
Scene list replaced, not filtered. While search is active the panel shows result snippets. Escape restores the scene list at its previous scroll position. Two distinct modes, one panel — the transition is instant, no animation.
2
Result anatomy: slug header + context snippet. The slug row reuses the same number gutter and INT./EXT. color vocabulary. The monospace snippet shows before · highlighted match · after. Grouped by scene so multiple hits in one scene stack under one header.
3
Two highlight intensities. The selected result (result 1, stronger pink) vs all other matches in the screenplay (ambient, soft pink). The screenplay shows both simultaneously — selected is the current anchor; ambient shows the full picture.
4
Results header: truth + escape. "3 results · 3 scenes" + "Esc to clear" — one line. No sort, no filter, no faceted controls. Results are ordered by screenplay position, not relevance — writers navigate spatially.
5
Clicking a result scrolls and highlights immediately. Navigation and highlight are one action. The clicked result becomes the strong highlight; the rest become ambient. A brief flash-confirm (~1s) on the screenplay line confirms the jump landed.

Large Script — 104 Scenes
At scale, rows step down to compact density (21px). Act group headers give structural orientation. Scene 47 (current) is auto-scrolled into view on load.
1
Compact density at scale. Past ~40 scenes, rows step from 26px → 21px. Location text stays legible at 10.5px. The number gutter and signal margin never compress — they are the list's skeleton and must hold at any density.
2
Act group headers give structural orientation. At 104 scenes the writer needs to know which act they're in at a glance. Group headers are derived from the document's structural markers — not manually managed by the writer.
3
Auto-scroll to current is the critical behavior at this scale. Scene 47 (pink rail) is in the viewport — because the navigator scrolls to it whenever the current scene changes. At 104 scenes, "you are here" must be visible without the writer doing anything.
4
Search is the primary scale solution. The compact list gives orientation; search gives precision retrieval. A character name, a location, a line of dialogue — the results panel surfaces the exact moment in under a second. Scrolling 104 rows is never the answer.

Interaction Notes
Expand / Collapse

Clicking the row navigates to the scene (existing behavior, unchanged). Clicking the expand chevron in the number gutter toggles expansion. Two distinct interaction targets — navigation and expansion never share the same click zone.

Expansion is instant — no animation. Multiple scenes may be expanded simultaneously. Keyboard: Space or → expands the selected row; ← or Space again collapses. ↑/↓ move selection; Enter navigates. Existing keyboard contract is unchanged.

Search Behavior

Focusing the search field enters search mode; the panel transitions to results immediately. Results update on every keystroke — no Enter required. Live, progressive, no loading state for typical scripts.

Results are ordered by screenplay position, not relevance score — writers navigate spatially, not by rank. Escape (1st press): clears the search text. Escape (2nd press): exits search mode entirely and restores the scene list at its previous scroll position.

Highlight + Sync

While search is active, all occurrences in the screenplay receive ambient highlight. The selected result receives the strong highlight. Two intensities; one truth.

Clicking any scene row, character anchor, or result item scrolls the screenplay immediately. A brief flash-confirm (~1s) on the target line confirms the jump. The current-scene indicator (pink rail) tracks the editor cursor continuously and independently — navigator and screenplay always share one "where you are," regardless of which mode is active.

Migration Notes
What carries forward unchanged

The row anatomy (number · heading · signals · page), the current/selected separation invariant, the brand-pink current rail, the amber monospace number gutter, and the reserved signal margin all carry forward intact. The row grid adds one column (the expand zone) but the visual logic and the separation invariant are untouched.

The existing "Find scene…" field is the physical container the new search inherits — same placement, same header, different scope. No removal; only upgrade.

The shell, rail, sidebar container, editor area, inspector, timeline, command palette, color system, and typographic system are untouched throughout. This is a navigator subsystem change only.

Change order, by priority

1. Auto-scroll current scene into view. The existing highest-value fix — keeps "you are here" visible. Ships first, independently. Zero schema change.

2. Full-text screenplay search. Replace slug-matching with screenplay-text search and the results panel. Ships independently of expand hierarchy — maximum value, minimum coupling.

3. Expandable scenes. Character anchors come from the existing screenplay parse. Marks from the existing note/flag model. No new per-scene schema fields required for the first cut.

4. Compact density + act groups. A CSS step-down at a scene-count threshold. Act groups need a structural act marker in the document model — gated behind that work, not blocked by it.
