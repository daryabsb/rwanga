## Decision 1 — Gesha's Character Development

**Question asked:** Is Gesha developed enough for the audience to feel for her? Should her diabetes be revealed earlier?

**Sarwar's answer:**

Yes — add one more subtle physical sign early in the film that hints at Gesha's diabetes. But the sign must be **subtle and physical**, not clinical. Insulin injections are too obvious and not useful. Instead, the hint should be something like:

- A change in complexion/skin color
- Eating (or avoiding) something specific

The key: the audience shouldn't consciously register "she has diabetes." They should notice *something* about her physical state that only makes sense in retrospect.

**Decision status:** Partially settled. The existing setup is insufficient — needs one more physical marker. The *type* of marker (complexion vs. food behavior) is still open.

**Decision status:** Settled. The mask does not recur. Existing beauty beats are sufficient. But a note is opened: find ways to deepen the audience's emotional attachment to Gesha before her death.

## Decision 3 — The Sherbet (Drink)

**Question asked:** Is the hint ("these will kill me") enough, or does the audience need more foreshadowing?

**Sarwar's answer:**

Add a **second hint** — a tea scene in the house. When Gesha makes tea, she says something like "don't put sugar in it." Nali notices and asks: "Why doesn't this girl eat sugar?" This creates a natural second data point without being heavy-handed.

**Darya's addition (marked by Sarwar as a "nice observation"):**

Gesha's relationship with sweetness should mirror her emotional state — specifically her closeness to/distance from Nali:

| Nali's behavior toward Gesha | Gesha's relationship with sugar | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Kind, close, warm | She **avoids** sweetness | Love for life increases — she protects herself |
| Cold, harsh, distant | She **gravitates toward** sweetness | Hopelessness — desire for death increases |

This creates a structural pattern: **distance from sweetness = love of life. Closeness to sweetness = surrender to death.**

This is not a symbol to be explained — it's a behavioral pattern the audience can feel across scenes without consciously tracking it.

**Decision status:** Settled with creative expansion. Two hints minimum (sherbet line + tea scene). Plus: Darya's sweetness-as-emotional-barometer pattern should be woven through the script as a behavioral throughline, not a one-off.

## Decision 4 — Gesha's Death Mechanism

**Question asked:** Is the death mechanism (cardiac arrest from sugar) believable without earlier medical clues?

**Sarwar's answer:**

Sarwar consulted diabetes specialists. The medical reality:

- If a diabetic person consumes excessive sugar and **falls into a diabetic coma** → it won't kill them in 1-2 hours (they can be rescued)
- If they consume excessive sugar and **don't fall into a coma** (i.e., remain conscious while hyperglycemia escalates) → it can kill them
- The dosage matters, but the script **doesn't need to specify the exact amount**
- The narrative explanation is sufficient: "She had diabetes, she ate sweets, she didn't use her medication, and it killed her"

**Decision status:** Settled. The death mechanism is medically plausible. No need for detailed medical exposition. The audience doesn't need to understand hyperglycemia — they need to understand: she had a condition, she consumed what she shouldn't have, she didn't take her medication, and it killed her.

## Creative Implications for the Chain

### What's now confirmed:
- Gesha's arc has **two layers of foreshadowing**: a physical diabetes sign (subtle, early) + the sweetness behavioral pattern (threaded through)
- The sherbet is **not a standalone hint** — it's part of a larger sweetness motif that maps to Gesha's emotional state
- The death needs **no medical exposition** — the narrative logic is sufficient
- The beauty mask is a **single-use motif**, not recurring

### What's still open:
- The specific nature of the early diabetes physical sign
- How Evin enters the story before the police station (surveillance is off, something else needed)
- How many sweetness-behavior beats to include and where exactly they fall in the scene structure

### Darya's sweetness pattern — structural note:
This is potentially the most important creative output from this chain. If implemented, it creates a **hidden emotional clock**: the audience unconsciously tracks Gesha's sugar consumption as a proxy for her will to live. When she reaches for the sherbet at the critical moment, it's not just a plot device — it's the culmination of a pattern they've been feeling without knowing it. This elevates the death from mechanical (she ate the wrong thing) to tragic (she chose to stop protecting herself).
