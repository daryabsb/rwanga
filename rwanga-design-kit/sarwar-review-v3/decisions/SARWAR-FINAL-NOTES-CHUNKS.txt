CHUNK 2 — NOTE 1: REMOVE OLD HOUSE
=====================================

Note 1 — Remove the Old House, Merge Into the Locked Room

Sarwar's direction: Remove the old house (dusty apartment) entirely from the script. Everything important that the apartment currently communicates — Nali's real life, the evidence of his dual existence — should be moved into the locked room.

The reasoning: the old house scenes take up screen time for a single narrative function (proving Nali's dual life at the police station). The locked room already carries mystery and tension. Loading it with the apartment's content makes the Scene 35 reveal stronger: when Nali opens the locked room, the audience discovers not just photos but an entire hidden life.

This change also saves runtime — directly addressing the producers' concern about film length.

=====================================
CHUNK 3 — NOTE 2: PRE-GARDEN THRILLER MOMENTS
=====================================

Note 2 — Add Thriller Moments Before Garden Arrival

Sarwar's exact words: "We need to scare the audience more pre-garden arrival. That is what the producers ask for. They need the audience to forget the safe sitting and watching the boring moments."

Darya's addition: "Until we reach the critical Gesha death moment. Then we can continue as the story flows, because now, all they care about is what will he do with the corpse! This is the scariest thing — everyone will create a scenario in their mind!"

Types of moments Sarwar wants:

1. Near-accident: Nali almost causes a car accident during the drive. Shows his confused, projection-driven mind. Nothing accidental — every incident must indicate something that comes later.

2. Environmental fear: A dead bird on the ground, a dog with a problem — small details that create unease. But each must have a reason and connect to something later in the film. No coincidence.

3. The 911 call (Darya's suggestion, endorsed by Sarwar): After Gesha dies and before Nali consults AI, he grabs his phone. He dials 911. Someone picks up: "What is the emergency?" He pauses. Looks at Gesha's body — beautiful, laid down as if asleep. He says: "Sorry, I thought I heard something but it was my dog." Hangs up. Then turns to AI for a solution.

The principle: everything that happens is an indicator of something that comes later. Fear must be earned, not decorative.

CHUNK 4 — NOTE 3: SENSUAL HINTS
=====================================

Note 3 — Add Sensual Hints and Moments

Context: Darya shared a personal production story — directing a music video for a friend's anthem-like song that nobody was listening to. The solution: bringing visual sensuality (performers in revealing outfits) that gave the audience a reason to stay engaged. The principle: sometimes sensual moments sell the product without cheapening it.

Darya's suggestion: "Showing some parts of Gesha — maybe she goes to the pool and puts her feet in the water." Also: "One lace of a bra can make a huge difference in marketing and grab more audience."

Sarwar's specific ideas:
- When Gesha gets changed in the other room, Nali looks at her shadow as she puts her clothes on.
- Nali tries to touch Gesha's hair once. She doesn't let him.
- Tara plays with Sozi's hair. Nali watches that sensual moment.
- Adib doesn't only have a problem with Tara's plastic surgery — he also has a problem with her sexy outfit. Sometimes she wears inappropriate things.

The rule: these moments must be behavioral, not explicit. No obvious nudity, no showing sensitive parts. Sexual hints and moves only — suggested, not displayed. The camera notices the way Nali notices: sideways, lingering a beat too long, never announced.

The purpose: making the audience physically aware of Gesha's presence so that her death becomes not just an emotional loss but a visceral one. Every sensual moment invested in Gesha alive pays off in horror when she is a corpse on the couch.
