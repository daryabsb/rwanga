# Bible Lens Prototype — 5 Scenes

**Exercise:** Take a scene excerpt (~200-500 words of screenplay), compress it to a ≤50 word analytical lens (what the bible section would say), then map which decisions govern this moment.

**Goal:** Validate the lens format before building the interactive viewer. Darya reviews whether the lens captures the analytical truth without losing the story.

## Scene 3 — Video Call with Gesha

### Screenplay Excerpt (~180 words)

During the day, Nali video-calls Gesha, a young woman he met online two months ago. She's wearing a beauty mask — the kind women use for skincare. They talk about masks — the ones people wear in society. Gesha is insistent: "If you delay, I'll never come." There's a moment where she glances off-camera — someone entered, or she heard something — and covers it fast. Her living situation isn't what she claims. After hanging up, Nali does what he always does: he calls his AI assistant. Not because he needs information. Because he needs certainty. AI tells him to stop reading people's minds and just meet her. Nali needs to hear this from a system that gives definitive answers, because humans don't.

### Lens (~48 words)

> **ماسک و دڵنیایی — دیمەنی ٣**
> 
> هەردوکیان دەشارنەوە. گەشە ماسکێکی جەستەیی لەبەردایە و ژیانی ڕاستەقینەی دەشارێتەوە. نالی دوای پەیوەندییەکە، بە AIیەوە "دڵنیایی" دەوێت — نەک زانیاری. ئەم دیمەنە یەکەم جار مۆدێلی پەیوەندیی نالی دەردەخات: ماسک لەگەڵ مرۆڤ، ڕاستی لە ئامێر.

### English Back-Translation (~48 words)

> Both are hiding. Gesha wears a physical mask and conceals her real life. Nali, after the call, seeks "certainty" from AI — not information. This scene first reveals Nali's relationship model: masks with humans, truth from machines.

### Mapped Decisions

- **D4** (AI role — observes, doesn't interpret)
- **D10** (Nali's core flaw — trusts interpretations from machines)
- **D15** (all interactions mediated)
- **D17** (certainty as fundamental problem)

## Scene 12 — Gesha Enters the House

### Screenplay Excerpt (~130 words)

Then Gesha enters the house. She's shocked by the interior — taxidermy heads, beauty masks, daggers, alien sculptures. It looks like a mad artist's museum. She discovers a paper on Nali's desk reading "Stories are waiting." She tries a locked door. It won't open. She stands there for a second. Leans in. Listens. What does she hear? Nothing. But the audience imagines everything. Nali deflects her questions casually. She goes to change clothes. The house is predatory and sad — it carries the weight of things that happened in it, or things Nali imagines happened. At night, it makes sounds: creaking, a drip, wind through a crack. The house remembers.

### Lens (~38 words) — *revised v3 per consultant*

> **سیستم وەک ژینگە — دیمەنی ١٢**
> 
> سیستمەکەی نالی خۆی وەک شوێن دەردەخات. ماڵەکە تەڵە نییە — فۆرمی فیزیکی کۆنترۆڵەکەیە. گەشە دەچێتە ناو بڕیارەکانی نالییەوە بەبێ ئەوەی بیزانێت. بینەر ئەمە دەزانێت. گەشە نازانێت.

### English Back-Translation (~38 words)

> Nali's system externalizes itself as environment. The house is not a trap — it is the physical form of his control. Gesha walks into Nali's decisions without knowing it. The audience knows this. Gesha doesn't.

### Mapped Decisions

- **D1** (controlled system with one hole)
- **D12** (dual-edge scenes — intimacy + threat escalate together)
- **D13** (audience smarter than Nali before midpoint)
- **D20↑** (house as part of the control system)

## Scene 16 — The Cruel Line + Sherbet

### Screenplay Excerpt (~200 words)

The game escalates. Gesha asks if Nali would stay with someone who has a terminal illness. He says yes, not knowing she's really asking about herself. Nali demands to see her phone. She can't show it. He demands to know why she came if she doesn't trust him. Nali breaks Gesha's heart with a cruel line: "You're the kind of girl who goes alone to meet a boy in a garden by herself?" It is a direct attack on her dignity and her decision to come.

Then Gesha eats the Shrubi. She eats out of anger, hopelessness, and heartbreak. Nali broke her heart. She didn't win him over. Her last card in life is burned. The act is driven by despair and functions as self-destructive behavior — Gesha is aware of the risk. She eats wildly, smearing the syrup on her face, but the abandon is not joy. It is surrender. A woman who has given up.

### Lens (~50 words)

> **هەرەسی دوولایەنە — دیمەنی ١٦**
> 
> نالی بە قسەیەکی ڕەق دڵی دەشکێنێت. گەشە لە بێئومێدیدا شڕوبییەکە دەخوات — دەزانێت دەیکوژێت. ئەمە خۆکوشتنە نەک خۆشی. دوو سیستمی تێکشکاو بەیەکدا دەکەون: پێویستیی نالی بۆ دڵنیایی، دوایین هیوای گەشەی وێران دەکات. بەیەکداکەوتنیان تراژیدیاکە دروست دەکات.

### English Back-Translation (~50 words)

> Nali breaks her heart with a cruel line. Gesha eats the sherbet in despair — she knows it will kill her. This is self-destruction, not joy. Two broken systems collide: Nali's need for certainty destroys Gesha's last hope. Their collision creates the tragedy.

### Mapped Decisions

- **D9↑** (sherbet = self-destruction in despair, not fun)
- **D12** (dual-edge scene — intimacy peak = threat peak)
- **D17** (certainty as core flaw — his cruelty comes from needing control)
- **New Principle: Bilateral Tragedy** (two broken systems collide)

## Scene 32 — The Locked Room

### Screenplay Excerpt (~120 words)

Then he opens the locked room. Inside: photographs of his past life in Europe — a man and woman who appear to be his parents. And a drawing — a portrait of a girl Nali loved in France. She looks very much like Gesha. This is why Nali wanted to get to know Gesha — because in his eyes, the French girl and Gesha are one and the same. He introduces dead Gesha to his old photos. No music. Just a man talking to a dead woman about his parents and the woman he lost before. Even in grief, the connection is mediated. Whether Nali is consciously aware of the resemblance is not specified.

### Lens (~42 words) — *revised v3 per consultant*

> **ئینجنی کاستینگ — دیمەنی ٣٢**
> 
> نالی مرۆڤەکان نابینێت — ڕۆڵ دروست دەکات. کچە فەرەنسییەکە ڕۆڵەکە بوو، گەشە نموونەکەی. ژوورە داخراوەکە ئینجنەکە ئاشکرا دەکات: نالی واقیع ناگۆڕێت — واقیع جێبەجێ دەکات. ئەمە دەروونناسی نییە. ئەمە ڕەفتاری سیستمە.

### English Back-Translation (~42 words)

> Nali does not perceive people — he instantiates roles. The French girl was the role, Gesha was the instance. The locked room reveals the engine: Nali doesn't replace reality — he executes it. This is not psychology. This is system behavior.

### Mapped Decisions

- **D17↑** (certainty — not addiction but an operating system for replacing reality)
- **D15** (mediation — memory-level: connection always through photographs, never direct)
- **Reality Replacement** (Nali replaces reality, doesn't misread it — this is the proof scene)
- **French Girl execution rules** (recognition must lag, but feeling must not)

## Scene 37 — The Desert / Leaving the Body

### Screenplay Excerpt (~160 words)

Nali drives with Gesha's body to a desert wasteland near overturned tankers — a landscape of things that crashed and were abandoned. He digs a hole. He tries to unlock her phone. It's dead. He buys a burner phone. But then he changes his mind. He can't bury her. He wraps her body and leaves it by a tree so her family can find her. He burns her phone and belongings but keeps her ID next to the body. This is Nali's most human moment since the evening with Gesha — his conscience operating without AI, without structure, without mediation. Direct human feeling breaking through the systems.

From a hilltop, he watches the ambulance lights find her body in the distance. He texts AI: "The hero left the body so the family could find her." AI is offline.

### Lens (~48 words)

> **مرۆییترین ساتی نالی — دیمەنی ٣٧**
> 
> نالی ناتوانێت بیشارێتەوە. ناسنامەکەی جێدەهێڵێت، کەلوپەلەکانی دەسووتێنێت. بۆ یەکەمجار بەبێ AI و بەبێ سیستەم بڕیار دەدات — ویژدانی ڕاستەقینە. دوای ئەوەش AIیەکە ئۆفلاینە. کاتژمێرەکە گەیشتە سفر. نالی بە تەواوی تەنیایە.

### English Back-Translation (~46 words)

> Nali can't bury her. He leaves her ID, burns her belongings. For the first time without AI, without any system, he makes a real decision — genuine conscience. Then AI goes offline. The hidden timer has reached zero. Nali is completely alone.

### Mapped Decisions

- **D5** (AI disappearance = hidden clock reaching zero)
- **D15** (mediation principle — this is the ONE moment it breaks)
- **D8** (the twist is revelation of process, not reversal)
- **New Principle: Reality Replacement** (on the hilltop, Nali sees Gesha in the bodies — his mind replacing reality)

## Pattern Observations

### What makes a good lens:

1. **≤50 words** — forces you to name the analytical truth, not retell the scene
2. **Names the mechanism** — not "what happens" but "what this reveals about the system"
3. **Uses the vocabulary of the decisions** — "دڵنیایی" (certainty), "ناڕاستەوخۆ" (mediated), "سیستم" (system), "خۆوێرانکردن" (self-destruction)
4. **Points forward or backward** — connects this moment to earlier/later moments in the chain
5. **Kurdish first** — the lens is in Kurdish because the director reads Kurdish. English back-translation is for development only.

### What the mapping reveals:

- D15 (mediation principle) maps to FOUR of these five scenes — it's the most pervasive structural rule
- D17 (certainty) maps to THREE scenes — it's the character spine
- Scene 16 (sherbet) is the densest moment — four decisions converge there
- Scene 37 is the inversion — the ONE moment where every rule breaks

### What the interactive viewer would show:

When Sarwar clicks D15 (ناڕاستەوخۆیی — mediation), scenes 3, 12, 16, 32, and 37 ALL highlight. But scene 37 highlights differently — it's the exception, the crack. The viewer should visually distinguish "this decision applies here" from "this is where this decision breaks."

### Scene 13 — Nali Alone in the Garden, Calling AI

#### Source Context (~300 words of screenplay, lines 985–1052)

Gesha goes to change clothes. Nali immediately pulls out his phone and calls AI in the garden. He talks about Gesha — he tested her about the incident, he's suspicious of her performance. AI tells him to stop reading people's minds, to ask questions about her desires, to find out who she'd be if she were free. Nali says AI's advice is "stale, old, and worthless." Then he sees Gesha has changed — she's wearing a short floral dress, her curly brown hair more beautiful. He hides the earphone, tells her he was talking to a "brother." Meanwhile, behind Nali, through the window, Gesha is visible changing — she doesn't know the window faces the garden. The scene ends with Nali complimenting her and trying to hold her hand.

#### Lens (~47 words)

> **مۆدێلی سیقەکردن — دیمەنی ١٣**
>
> گەشە دەچێت خۆی بگۆڕێت. نالی یەکسەر تەلەفۆن بۆ AI دەکات — نەک بۆ یارمەتی، بەڵکو بۆ پشتڕاستکردنەوەی گومانەکانی. AI پێی دەڵێت "واز لە خوێندنەوەی مێشکی مرۆڤەکان بهێنە." نالی گوێ نادات. ئەم دیمەنە دەردەخات: نالی AI وەک ئامێری کۆنترۆڵ بەکاردێنێت، نەک وەک ڕابەر.

#### English Back-Translation (~46 words)

> Gesha goes to change. Nali immediately calls AI — not for help, but to confirm his suspicions. AI says "stop reading people's minds." Nali ignores it. This scene reveals: Nali uses AI as a control instrument, not as a guide.

#### Mapped Decisions

- **D4** (AI role — observes, doesn't interpret) — Nali violates this, forcing AI into interpretation
- **D10** (Nali's core flaw — trusts machine interpretations over human signals)
- **D15** (mediation principle — his FIRST reaction after being alone with a real person is to call AI)
- **D17** (certainty — he needs AI to confirm what he already suspects)

### Scene 21 — Rehearsal Over the Body

#### Source Context (~500 words of screenplay, lines 2282–2520)

Nali, Nazi, and Edib arrive at the house. Nazi finds the sherbet tray on the table and teases Nali about having a guest. Edib asks to change his co-actor (Tara has had too much cosmetic surgery, he can't feel anything performing opposite her). They rehearse Chekhov's *The Seagull* — Nina and Triplyov's dialogue — while Nali sits on the couch that has Gesha's body underneath. The camera focuses on Nali's face while the actors perform. Nali keeps glancing at the couch. Edib says the lines: "Life should be shown as it is in our dreams, not as it actually is." Nali stares at the couch where the body is. He's absent. Edib notices. Nazi performs well. They discuss blocking, Nali suggests moving to the other side of the room — away from the couch. Edib smokes by the window, praises Nazi's performance. The scene ends with Nali silently staring at the body-couch, smoking, looking out at the garden.

#### Lens (~44 words) — *rewritten per consultant: pure rule definition*

> **لووتکەی ناڕاستەوخۆیی — دیمەنی ٢١**
>
> کاتێک ناڕاستەوخۆیی دەگاتە لووتکەکەی، دەبێتە هونەر. نالی تەرمەکە ناشارێتەوە — نواندنێک بەڕێوە دەبات کە تەرمەکە نابینراو دەکات. دەرهێنەر و بکوژ یەک ڕۆڵن. ئەمە D15 لە فۆرمی تەواویدایە: ناڕاستەوخۆیی وەک هونەر.

#### English Back-Translation (~44 words)

> When mediation reaches its peak, it becomes art. Nali doesn't hide the body — he stages a performance that makes the body invisible. Director and killer are the same role. This is D15 in its complete form: mediation as art.

#### Mapped Decisions

- **D1** (controlled system with one hole) — the couch is the hole, every moment someone might find it
- **D12** (dual-edge scenes) — rehearsal is art and horror simultaneously
- **D13** (audience smarter than Nali before midpoint) — the audience sees the irony Edib can't
- **D15 → Artistic Mediation (Level 4)** — the peak expression of mediation; art replaces reality management

### Scene 34 — The Desert Burial Attempt

#### Source Context (~200 words of screenplay, lines 3233–3250)

Nali arrives at a flat desert near overturned tankers. He parks, takes out a shovel and pickaxe, starts digging a hole in dry, barren ground. He keeps looking around nervously. After a while, he stops. Goes to the car, lights a cigarette. Something crosses his mind. He goes to the trunk, opens the coffin, pulls Gesha's body halfway out, and stops — if he buries her, his conscience will never leave him, he'll be tormented forever. A look of regret on his face. He goes back toward the hole. Lights a fire. Takes Gesha's phone — locked, but he can read half the incoming messages through notifications. Someone is worried, asking her to come home. He burns the phone and belongings in the fire but places her ID next to the body. Watches the coffin through the car window.

#### Lens (~50 words)

> **ویژدان بەبێ سیستم — دیمەنی ٣٤**
>
> نالی چاڵ هەڵدەکەنێت بەڵام ناتوانێت گەشە بشارێتەوە. ناسنامەکەی دەهێڵێت، کەلوپەلەکانی دەسووتێنێت. لە نامەکانی مۆبایلەکەوە ئەوکەسانە دەناسێت کە بەدوای گەشەدا دەگەڕێن. بۆ یەکەمجار بڕیارەکەی ڕاستەوخۆیە — نە AI، نە نواندن، نە ئامێر. تەنها مرۆڤ و ویژدان.

#### English Back-Translation (~48 words)

> Nali digs a hole but can't bury Gesha. He leaves her ID, burns her belongings. From the phone's notifications, he sees the people searching for her. For the first time, his decision is direct — no AI, no performance, no instrument. Just a human and his conscience.

#### Mapped Decisions

- **D5** (AI disappearance = hidden clock reaching zero) — AI is unreachable in the desert, no internet
- **D8** (the twist is revelation of process, not reversal) — nothing reverses; we simply see the human beneath the system
- **D15** (mediation principle — this is where it BREAKS) — first unmediated decision
- **D17** (certainty) — Nali can't get certainty from anyone; he must act alone
- **New Principle: Reality Replacement** — through phone messages, Nali finally meets the real Gesha he never knew

## Comparative Analysis: Round 1 vs Round 2

### What changed with real screenplay source:

1. **Dialogue gives the lens teeth.** Scene 13's lens uses AI's actual words ("واز لە خوێندنەوەی مێشکی مرۆڤەکان بهێنە" — stop reading people's minds) as the pivot. The Story Bible version was more abstract.

2. **Physical detail anchors the mechanism.** Scene 21's lens works because of the SPECIFIC image: actors rehearsing on top of a body. The Chekhov dialogue ("life as it is in our dreams") becomes devastating irony. This level of concrete detail wasn't available from the prose summary.

3. **The summary version (Sarwar's 14-page treatment) compresses differently than our lens.** Sarwar's summary is plot-focused — it tells you WHAT happens. Our lens is mechanism-focused — it tells you WHAT THIS REVEALS about the system. Both compress ~300-500 words to ~50, but they compress along different axes.

### Reclassification — Art as Cover → D15 Level 4 *(per consultant)*

The consultant's verdict: "Art as Cover" is **NOT** a new decision. It is the **peak expression** of D15 (mediation). Making it Decision 26 would fragment the system. Keeping it inside D15 deepens it — showing escalation:

**D15 — Mediation (Extended)**
- Level 1: AI mediation (Scenes 3, 13)
- Level 2: Social/communicative mediation (Scene 16)
- Level 3: Memory mediation (Scene 32)
- Level 4: **Artistic mediation** (Scene 21) — the peak form
- Level 0: **Broken** (Scenes 34, 37) — the system crack

This hierarchy will drive the interactive viewer: when a user clicks D15, each scene highlights differently based on its expression level.

### Decision coverage with Expression Types:

| Scene | D15 Expression | D17 Expression | Other Decisions |
|-------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| Sc.3  | emotional (AI for certainty) | active (seeking) | D4, D10 |
| Sc.12 | — | — | D1, D12, D13, D20↑ |
| Sc.13 | behavioral (AI as control) | active (confirming suspicion) | D4, D10 |
| Sc.16 | — | weaponized (cruelty from need) | D9↑, D12, Bilateral Tragedy |
| Sc.21 | **artistic** (peak form) | — | D1, D12, D13 |
| Sc.32 | memory (photos, never direct) | operating system (casting logic) | Reality Replacement |
| Sc.34 | **broken** (first direct act) | absent (no system to consult) | D5, D8, Reality Replacement |
| Sc.37 | **broken** (one real moment) | absent | D5, D8, Reality Replacement |

**D15 maps to 6 of 8 scenes** — not over-applied, but appearing *differently each time*. That's the real test (per consultant): not frequency, but variation.

**D17 maps to 5 of 8 scenes** — the character spine, with distinct expression types from "seeking" to "weaponized" to "absent."
