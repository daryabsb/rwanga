## What happened

Darya is building the Rwanga platform (Kurdish cinema preproduction). The project is "Mysterious Guest" (میوانێکی نادیار) directed by Sarwar. We're in the bible review process — the director reviews analytical decisions about the story's structure and locks/rejects them.

### The consultant exercise

We ran a lens prototype exercise with Sarwar (who is both the director and the consultant). A "lens" is a ≤50 word analytical summary that defines the RULE a scene obeys, not what happens in it. We validated this format across 8 scenes and built a D15 mediation chain (5 scenes showing how one decision evolves across the film).

Key rule from the consultant: **"A lens should not describe the scene. It should define the rule the scene obeys."**

### The chain pivot

Darya made a strategic decision: instead of continuing the consultant's 5-scene picks (which were based on locked decisions), we pivot to unsettled decisions from the v03 review. Sarwar hasn't seen the v03 decisions yet. Building chains for unsettled decisions serves double duty — validates the chain methodology AND helps him settle decisions structurally.

### What we gave Sarwar

A 3-page branded HTML brief (`SARWAR-CHAIN-BRIEF.html`) explaining:
- What chains are (groups of load-bearing decisions)
- Why they matter (forces honest answers, shows dependencies, validates the format)
- Why pick one first (teaches the method, his feedback shapes the rest)
- Four chain options to choose from

He picks one letter (A, B, C, or D). Darya gives him the detailed chain document for that pick. He reads, reacts, Darya captures.

## The Four Chains

### Chain A — Gesha: The Guest's Own Tragedy (5 decisions)

**Decision IDs in Rwanga:**
1. `76487c5e` — Gesha's character + diabetes
2. `c560c259` — Skincare mask motif
3. `ad235c7e` — Sherbet: nostalgia → death instrument
4. `b8842d96` — Gesha's death mechanism (cardiac arrest from sugar)
5. `395963bc` — Evin reveals Gesha's truth at police station

**Core question:** Does the audience understand Gesha was doomed by her OWN story, not just by Nali's?

**Scene trace:** Scene 3 (video call, mask) → Scene 8-10 (travel, sherbet in car, "these will kill me") → Scene 16 (cruel line, Gesha eats sherbet in despair = self-destruction) → death overnight → Scene 38-39 (Evin's revelation at police station)

**Key structural argument:** The sherbet decision can't be settled alone because it connects to: diabetes (must the audience know earlier?), death mechanism (is cardiac arrest from sugar believable?), Evin (does her exposition dump work or need seeding?). If the hint is enough → diabetes can stay hidden → Evin's reveal is a proper surprise. If the hint is NOT enough → diabetes needs earlier signs → Evin might need to appear before the police station.

These three together ask: is the physical world a character in this film, or is it overloaded?

### Chain C — The Film's Architecture: How the Frame Works (5 decisions)

**Decision IDs in Rwanga:**
1. `3e31d9a6` — The final twist (all was Nali's imagination)
2. `e0ae64c7` — Opening montage (meaningless fragments → meaningful at end)
3. `af33d5e1` — Chekhov's Seagull as mirror of main story
4. `6089f2c7` — The ending (Nali answers phone without system)
5. `eeece2b4` — The gardener (framing device, no time has passed)

**Core question:** Do the last 5 minutes make the previous 90 minutes richer or cheaper?

**Scene trace:** Scene 1 (montage: grave, bride burning, body, car crash, tunnel police) → Scene 2 (gardener starts talking) → Scenes 21-24 (Chekhov rehearsal on the body) → Scene 42 (twist: finger isn't cut, back in garden, it was all imagination) → Scene 43 (Gesha calls, alive, Nali answers without system — gardener still talking)

**Key structural argument:** The twist reframes EVERYTHING. If it feels like a cheat, the film collapses. If it feels earned, it elevates the film from thriller to psychological study. The opening montage is the setup for the payoff — each fragment (grave = the burial attempt, car crash = Edib's death, tunnel police = the checkpoint). The gardener is the temporal anchor. Chekhov is the thematic mirror. The ending is the resolution question. These five decisions determine whether the audience walks out thinking "that was brilliant" or "that was a trick."

Is Truth or Dare too transparent as interrogation? Is Edib's death mechanism too convenient? Is the desert collapse earned? These are the nuts and bolts that make the engine run.

## Key rules for new sessions

1. **Lenses in English only.** Darya's translator handles Kurdish.
2. **A lens defines the rule, not the scene.** "When mediation reaches its peak, it becomes art" not "actors rehearse Chekhov on the couch."
3. **Chains show evolution, not just presence.** Each link must have type, intensity, function, and transition to next link.
4. **Darya runs the process.** Don't treat this as a two-party conversation between AI and consultant. Everything goes through Darya.
5. **Brand format:** Use Rwanga export templates (`rwanga-design-kit/templates/exports/`) for any documents. Pink (#F72585), amber (#D4A574), Cairo + Inter fonts.
