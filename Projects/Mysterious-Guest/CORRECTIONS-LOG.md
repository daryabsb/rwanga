## Category A — Factual Corrections

These are errors in our bible/review caused by working from an outdated screenplay version. Each entry shows what our documents say vs. what the current screenplay actually contains.

### A1. Video Call Timing (Scene 3)

- **Old (our bible):** "That evening, Nali video-calls Gesha" — Scene 3 listed as "Evening"
- **Corrected:** The video call happens during the day, not evening/night.

### A2. The Mask (Scene 3)

- **Old (our bible):** "She's wearing a green herbal face mask" — described in context of theatrical masks metaphor. Scene 12 describes "theatrical masks" in the house. Our analysis treated the mask as thematically linked to Nali's theater identity.
- **Corrected:** The mask is a facial beauty mask (دەمووچاو) — the kind women use for skincare. It is a cosmetic/domestic object, not a theatrical one.

### A3. Café Scene Removed — Awin Meets Nali at Police Precinct

- **Old (our bible):** "Scene 41 — Awin at the Cafe. Nali meets Awin privately." Listed in scene table as "41 | Cafe | Day"
- **Corrected:** The café scene with Awin no longer exists. Awin tells Nali about Gesha's life at the police precinct (بنکەی پۆلیس), not at a separate café meeting.

### A4. Why Nali Goes to the Precinct

- **Old (our bible):** "Scene 39 — The Police. Officers come to Nali's old, dusty apartment and ask him to come to the station regarding both Adib's death and Gesha's disappearance."
- **Corrected:** Nali is called to the precinct because Adib was last located at Nali's garden — his friends left him there. Police are tracing Adib's last known location. When Nali arrives at the precinct, he recognizes Awin (coincidentally). The investigation then splits into two: one for Adib, one for Gesha. The encounter with Awin is not prearranged.

### A5. Adib's Presence at the Garden

- **Old (our bible):** Adib arrives with Nazi, Ako, and Tara as part of the theater group (Scene 20+). He dies in Scene 29 after finding the body.
- **Corrected (additional detail):** After Scene 29, Adib's friends are the ones who ultimately left Adib at Nali's garden. This is why police contact Nali — they're tracing Adib's last known location. (Note: the sequence of events around Adib's death and the friends' departure may need further clarification from Sarwar.)

## Category B — Behavioral Clarifications

These are not factual errors but corrections to how we interpreted character behavior. The events exist in our bible, but we described the motivation incorrectly or insufficiently.

### B1. Nali's Cruel Line to Gesha

- **What our bible says:** The Truth or Dare sequence escalates tension. Nali demands Gesha's phone. Gesha gets angry.
- **What Sarwar specified:** There is a specific line of dialogue where Nali breaks Gesha's heart with cruel words: "تۆ چۆن کچێکی کە بەتەنیا ئەچیت کوڕێک ببینیت لە باخێکدا بەتەنیا ؟" — "You're the kind of girl who goes alone to meet a boy in a garden by herself?"
- **What this means:** This is the specific moment that wounds Gesha. It is a direct verbal attack on her dignity and her decision to come.

### B2. Shrubi — Motive for Consumption

- **What our bible says:** "Gesha eats the Shrubi sweets excessively — smearing them on her face like a performer, dancing, laughing wildly. It's joyful but unhinged." Our analysis treated this as a suspense mechanism (Chekhov's gun) and behavioral emphasis (Decision 9).
- **What Sarwar specified:** Gesha drinks/eats the Shrubi out of anger, hopelessness, and heartbreak (توڕەبوونەوە و بێ هیوای). Nali broke her heart. She didn't win him over. Her last card in life is burned. It is as if she wants to commit suicide (وەک ئەوەی بیەوێ خۆی ئینتیحار بکات). The Shrubi consumption is a self-destructive act born from emotional collapse, not wild joy.
- **What this means:** The "joyful but unhinged" description in our bible is wrong in tone. The correct emotional register is despair masked as abandon. The act is driven by despair and functions as self-destructive behavior — Gesha is aware of the risk. She has given up.

### B3. Nali Projecting Gesha onto the Garden Corpses

- **What our bible says:** Scene 37 — "Watches ambulance find body from distance." No mention of Nali seeing a sign that reminds him of Gesha.
- **What Sarwar specified:** When Nali watches the corpses at his garden from a distance, he sees a sign/signal (نیشانەیەک بە حاڵ) that makes him feel it might be Gesha. This is shown to illustrate Nali's disturbed mental state (حاڵەتی شێواوی نالی).
- **What this means:** Nali interprets unrelated visual signals as connected to Gesha. This is a specific distortion event, not gradual deterioration.

## Category C — Structural Additions

These are elements not present in our current bible/review at all. They represent either new material from Sarwar or content from a newer screenplay version we haven't seen.

### C1. The French Girl in the Locked Room

- **What our bible says:** "He opens the locked room for the first time in the film. Inside: photographs of his past life in Europe — with a man and woman who appear to be his parents. He introduces the dead Gesha to his old photos, a heartbreaking gesture of connection." The locked room section says: "Contains Nali's real identity — his European past, his parents."
- **What Sarwar specified:** In the locked room, there is a drawing/portrait (رەسمێک) that reveals the girl Nali loved in France looks very much like Gesha (زۆر لە گەشە ئەچێت). This is why Nali wanted to get to know Gesha — because in his eyes, the French girl and Gesha are one and the same (کچەکەی فەرەنسا و گەشە یەکێکن).
- **Status:** Not present in our current bible. Either from a newer screenplay draft or a new idea that emerged from Sarwar reading our reviews. Darya confirms this is the first time Sarwar has mentioned it.
- **What this means (factual only):** The locked room contains evidence of a prior romantic attachment. Gesha's resemblance to this woman is Nali's underlying reason for pursuing her. The Paris story in Scene 10 ("a woman with yellow hair who drank coffee and stared at him") may be an early, indirect reference to this same woman. The screenplay presents this connection; the level of Nali's conscious awareness of the resemblance is not specified.
