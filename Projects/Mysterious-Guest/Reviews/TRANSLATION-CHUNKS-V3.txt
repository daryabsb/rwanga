The projection onto the garden corpses (Scene 37) confirms this. When Nali sees a signal that makes him feel the body might be Gesha, he is not deteriorating gradually — he is experiencing a specific distortion event. His mind overwrites reality. New Principle: Bilateral Tragedy

[60]
"Two broken systems collide. The tragedy is shared."

[61]
Gesha is not material for Nali's reconstruction. She has her own desperation, her own last card, her own collapse. She enters Nali's system carrying her own reasons — none of which have anything to do with him. The film's deepest cut is that a real person would have walked into his constructed reality and been destroyed inside it — not by him specifically, but because the system cannot contain both truths. His need for certainty. Her need for love. Incompatible under the same roof.

The French Girl — Execution Rules

[63]
Rule: Recognition Must Be Delayed — Resonance Must Not Be

[64]
The audience must feel the pattern before they understand it. No explicit early exposition. No clear "he lost someone like her." But: visual and emotional echoing early. The Paris story (Scene 10) plants the resonance. The locked room portrait (Scene 32) delivers the recognition.

[65]
If revealed too late with no prior echo: feels like explanation. If revealed too early: feels like motivation simplification. Correct structure: Early = unconscious repetition signals. Mid = discomfort. Late = confirmation.

[66]
Rule: The French Girl Must Destabilize, Not Explain

[67]
After the reveal, the audience should think: "Is he repeating? Is he projecting? Is Gesha real as herself?" — NOT: "Oh, he misses his ex." The French girl should not explain Nali. She should destabilize the audience's interpretation of everything they've watched.

[68]
Awareness boundary: Whether Nali is consciously aware of the resemblance is not specified in the screenplay. This ambiguity must be preserved — it is a feature, not a gap.

[69]
How All 20 Decisions + Upgrades Work Together

[70]
Layer 1 — What the film IS (D1, D11, D19, D20 upgraded): A psychologically suspenseful, architecturally sound film whose core tension is between constructed reality and direct human contact. The house, AI, and game are a single system. The environment itself is part of the mediation.

[71]
Layer 2 — How the characters work (D10, D14, D15, D17 upgraded): Nali is addicted to certainty and is recreating a previous emotional structure. All his interactions are mediated. He must remain behaviorally grounded. Gesha carries her own collapse — the tragedy is bilateral.

[72]
Layer 3 — How the story mechanics work (D2, D3, D5, D7, D8, D9 upgraded, D12, D13): Dual-function scenes. Single dominant bomb per phase. Audience smarter than Nali before midpoint. Sofa protected by upstream tension. Twist is revelation, not reversal. Shrubi is decision under collapse — despair, not joy.

[73]
Layer 4 — How the tools behave (D4, D6, D16, D18): AI observes but doesn't interpret. Scenes compress, don't cut. One twist, not many. Recognition delayed, resonance not.

[74]
New principles: Nali replaces reality, doesn't misinterpret it. The tragedy is bilateral — two broken systems colliding. The French girl destabilizes interpretation, doesn't simplify it.
