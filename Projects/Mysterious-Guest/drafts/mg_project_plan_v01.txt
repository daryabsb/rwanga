The Package: Scene 12 Preproduction Kit

4. Shot List (Technical) - The production-ready version of the breakdown: shot number, setup number (grouped by camera position to minimize moves), lens, camera movement, duration estimate, sound notes, lighting notes. This is what the DP and AD work from on set.
5. Shooting Schedule / Stripboard - The shots reordered by camera setup, not script order. Groups shots that share the same camera position so the crew isn't moving equipment back and forth. Includes estimated time per setup.
6. Props List - Every physical object that needs to be on set: the deer head, theater masks (quantity, style), daggers, swords, sculptures, the laptop, the "Stories are waiting" paper, bags/suitcase for Gesha, meat for the dog. Status column: have it / need to source / need to make.

8. Location Requirements Checklist - What the location needs to have or we need to bring: power supply, room dimensions that match blocking, wall space for hanging props, two doors (one lockable), kitchen counter area, desk area, natural light source for the evening amber look. Scout checklist format.
9. Lighting Plan - Based on Sarwar's answers about mood: general lighting direction, practical lights on set, time of day considerations, any gels or diffusion needed. Tied to the floor plan.
10. Sound Notes - Dialogue-heavy scene, so: boom vs. lav decisions per shot, ambient sound considerations (garden sounds through windows?), any practical sound effects (door handle, bags being set down, footsteps on specific flooring).
11. Call Sheet Template - Ready to fill in once date and crew are confirmed: location address, crew list with call times, cast list with call times and makeup/wardrobe times, parking, emergency contacts, sunrise/sunset, weather.
12. Continuity Notes - What happens before (Scene 11: they arrive at the garden, the dog greets them) and after (Scene 13: Nali feeds the dog, walks in the garden, talks to AI). What must carry over in both directions.
13. Risk & Contingency Notes - What could go wrong and the backup plan: weather affecting light through windows, prop damage, actor availability, equipment failure, time overrun on complex setups like the tracking shot.
