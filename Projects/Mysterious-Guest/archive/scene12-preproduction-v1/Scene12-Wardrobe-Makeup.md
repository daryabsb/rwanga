## NALI — Wardrobe

### Look Concept

Nali is a 38-year-old playwright and theater director. He's artistic but not flamboyant. His clothes are comfortable, slightly rumpled from a long day of driving and hosting. He dresses like a man who cares about texture and quality but not about trends. Think: a European art-house director on his day off. Earthy tones, natural fabrics.

### Outfit

| Item | Description | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Shirt | Casual button-up in natural linen or soft cotton. Color: muted earth tone — olive, dusty sage, warm grey, or soft khaki. NOT white (too bright for amber lighting). Slightly wrinkled is fine — he's been wearing it all day. | Sleeves can be rolled to mid-forearm (shows casualness). Top button undone. |
| Trousers | Relaxed fit chinos or linen trousers. Color: dark earth — charcoal, deep olive, dark brown. | Not jeans — Nali is too deliberate for denim. Not dress pants — he's at his country house. |
| Shoes | Leather sandals, worn loafers, or simple slip-ons. | Seen only in wide shots. Nothing shiny or new. |
| Belt | Worn leather belt, or no belt. | If trousers fit well, skip the belt — cleaner silhouette. |
| Watch | Optional — a simple, older watch with a leather strap. Not smart watch. | Adds character detail. A man who owns a watch, not a phone-checker (ironic given his AI relationship). |
| Accessories | None on body. His "accessories" are the house itself. | No rings, no necklace, no bracelets. Nali is deliberately stripped-down. |

### Multiples

| Item | Quantity | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Shirt | 2 identical | In case of stains, sweat, or continuity reset. Linen wrinkles differently each wear — break in both shirts identically before shoot day. |
| Trousers | 1 (+ backup) | Less likely to need replacement. Bring iron for touch-ups between setups. |

### Continuity from Scene 11

Nali has been wearing this outfit since Scene 7 (picking up Gesha, driving through the city, buying sweets, passing checkpoints). By Scene 12, the shirt should show natural wear: slight creasing at the elbows, the front slightly untucked on one side. Do NOT press and reset between Scenes 11 and 12 — the lived-in quality is the point. Photograph the exact state of the shirt after Scene 11 wrap for matching.

## GESHA — Wardrobe

### Look Concept

Gesha is in her early-to-mid 20s. She met Nali online two months ago. This is their first in-person overnight visit — she's dressed to impress but not over-the-top. She's from a modest background (working hard to pay for insulin, as revealed later in the story). Her clothes are nice but not expensive. She has a natural, slightly bohemian taste. She's also practical — she's going to a remote garden house, not a gala.

### Outfit A (Enters Scene 12 — worn since Scene 6)

| Item | Description | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Top | Dark top — black or very dark navy. Simple cut: crew neck or slight V-neck. Fitted but not tight. Could be a soft knit or cotton blouse. | Must work with the amber interior lighting — dark fabric absorbs light and makes her face the brightest element in frame. This is intentional. |
| Bottom | Casual pants or fitted jeans. Color: dark — black, dark blue, or charcoal. | Not a skirt/dress — she's been in a car for hours and walking through a market. Practical. |
| Shoes | Comfortable flats, low boots, or casual sneakers. | She's been on her feet. Nothing that looks uncomfortable. |
| Jacket/Layer | Optional — a light jacket or cardigan she removes as she enters the warm house. Removing a layer = settling in, a subtle vulnerability gesture. | If used, she can drape it over a chair when she puts bags on the dining table — adds natural blocking. |
| Sunglasses | Pushed up on head (as in reference photo). | She wore them during the drive. Now they sit on her head — she might forget they're there. A small, real detail. |
| Jewelry | Minimal — small earrings, maybe a simple necklace or bracelet. Nothing flashy. | Gesha is not a jewelry person. One or two pieces max that she always wears. |

### Outfit B (Changes into at end of Scene 12 — seen in Scene 14)

| Item | Description | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Dress | Short floral dress (per screenplay — "Gesha returns in a short floral dress" in Scene 14). Casual, slightly romantic. Floral pattern in warm tones or mixed colors. Knee-length or just above. | This is her "evening" look — what she packed to wear for dinner. It signals: she's trying, she wants to look nice for him. |
| Shoes | Can change to sandals or remain in the same shoes from Outfit A. | Not critical — shots in Scene 14 are mostly seated. |

### Why the Change Matters

The wardrobe change is a story beat. Outfit A = the road, the practical, the guarded Gesha who just walked into a strange house. Outfit B = the evening Gesha, the one choosing to stay, to open up, to play Truth or Dare. The audience should feel the shift even if they don't consciously register the costume change.

### Multiples

| Item | Quantity | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Dark top (Outfit A) | 2 identical | Sweat, stains, continuity resets. |
| Floral dress (Outfit B) | 2 identical | Scene 14 involves cooking and pool — high risk of damage. Not needed for Scene 12 shoot day but should be sourced now for full production readiness. |

### Continuity from Scene 11

Same as Nali — she's been in Outfit A since the market (Scene 6). The clothes show a day of wear. Hair may be slightly wind-affected from the garden arrival. Do NOT reset to fresh for Scene 12 — she walked directly from the garden into the house. Photograph state at Scene 11 wrap.

## GESHA — Makeup & Hair

### Hair

| Detail | Direction |
|--------|-----------|
| Style | Long, curly, honey-blonde, loose and natural. As per reference photo. Falling past shoulders. Not styled into any particular shape — this is her natural texture. |
| State in Scene 12 | Slightly wind-touched from the outdoor scenes (11). Not messy, but not salon-perfect. A few strands across her face that she might tuck behind her ear during close-ups. |
| Product | Minimal. Anti-frizz if needed for the curls to hold shape under warm lights. No hairspray — the hair should move naturally when she turns her head. |
| Sunglasses | Pushed up on top of her head, sitting in the curls. She might remove them at some point during the scene — coordinate with director. If she removes them, she needs a place to put them (table, bag). |
| Continuity | Photograph from front and both sides after Scene 11. The specific fall of her curls — which side has more volume, where the part sits — must match. |

### Skin / Makeup

| Detail | Direction |
|--------|-----------|
| Foundation | Light, natural coverage. Gesha wears makeup but it should look like she barely tried — "no-makeup makeup." Even out skin tone, nothing more. |
| Eyes | Minimal — light mascara, no heavy liner or shadow. Her green-hazel eyes are a story asset (they catch the amber light beautifully). Don't obscure them with product. In the close-up (12.3), we need to see the COLOR of her irises. |
| Lips | Natural tone or a barely-there tinted balm. Nothing glossy — gloss catches light unpredictably and becomes distracting in close-ups. |
| Blush | None, or the faintest warm tone on cheekbones. The amber lighting will add warmth naturally. |
| Brows | Natural, groomed but not shaped. Gesha doesn't go to a brow bar. |
| Continuity | By Scene 12, she's been wearing this makeup since morning (in-story). It should look slightly settled — not freshly applied. A tiny bit of mascara migration under the eyes is REAL and good. Don't correct it unless it's distracting. |

### Emotional Makeup Notes

Gesha's face must be readable across a range of emotions in this scene: fascination, unease, curiosity, fear, forced calm, suspicion. Heavy makeup works against all of this. The less product on her face, the more the camera sees. Trust the actress's face and the lighting.

## Quick-Reference: Side-by-Side Comparison

| | Nali | Gesha |
|---|------|-------|
| Overall Tone | Earthy, comfortable, artistic | Casual, natural, modest |
| Colors | Muted earth (olive, sage, charcoal) | Dark top (black/navy), dark bottoms |
| Fabric | Linen, soft cotton | Cotton, knit, denim |
| Wear Level | End-of-day wrinkled, lived-in | End-of-day, natural |
| Hair | Swept back, loosened | Long curls, free, natural |
| Makeup | Powder for shine only | No-makeup makeup, light mascara |
| Key Visual | He blends INTO the house (earth tones match the room) | She stands OUT from the house (dark clothing against warm amber room) |

The last point is the most important design choice: Nali's wardrobe makes him part of the house. Gesha's wardrobe makes her a visitor. He belongs here. She doesn't. The audience should feel this contrast without thinking about it.
