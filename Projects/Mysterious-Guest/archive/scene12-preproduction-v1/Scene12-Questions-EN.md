# Questions for Meeting with Sarwar — Scene 12

**Mysterious Guest** — Test Shoot Preproduction

---

## Section 1: About the Shot Proposal

1. We sent you a 15-shot proposal for Scene 12, divided into three movements (Entrance, Discovery, Locked Door). Did you read it? What's your first reaction?

2. Are there any shots you want to remove, add, or change completely?

3. The original breakdown had 8 shots. We expanded it to 15 to capture more of the screenplay's emotional beats. Is that too many for a test shoot, or do you want to shoot all 15?

4. Do you agree with the three-movement structure, or do you see the scene differently in your head?

---

## Section 2: Director's Vision — Tone & Feeling

5. When you close your eyes and imagine this scene, what do you feel? Is it horror? Suspense? Dark romance? Something else?

6. How fast or slow should this scene breathe? Should the audience feel suffocated or should there be moments of air?

7. The house is almost a third character in this scene. How alive do you want it to feel? Should it feel like it's watching Gesha?

8. When Gesha sees the deer head — is it a jump scare moment, or a slow creeping realization?

9. The locked door — how much weight do you want it to carry? A passing detail, or a moment the audience should remember?

---

## Section 3: Camera & Lighting

10. Do you have a preference for camera movement style? Handheld (documentary feel), steadicam (floating/dreamy), or dolly/track (controlled/cinematic)?

11. What's the lighting mood? Warm amber throughout, or should it shift as Gesha discovers more unsettling things?

12. Any reference films or directors whose visual style you want to channel for this scene? (e.g., Villeneuve, Tarkovsky, Farhadi, a Kurdish film?)

13. Do you want any specific lens choices — wide angle to distort the space, or longer lenses to compress and feel claustrophobic?

---

## Section 4: Actors & Performance

14. Have you cast Nali and Gesha for the test shoot? If yes, who are they? If not, what are you looking for?

15. How should Nali behave in this scene — is he actively performing for Gesha (showing off his world), or is he genuinely casual and unaware of how strange his house looks?

16. Gesha's fear — should it be visible on her face, or should she try to hide it? Is she the type to show vulnerability or mask it?

17. Do you want the dialogue delivered exactly as written in the screenplay, or are you open to improvisation?

---

## Section 5: Location & Props

18. Do you have the actual location for the garden house? Can we scout it before the shoot?

19. The taxidermy deer head — do you have one, or do we need to source/make it? How realistic does it need to be?

20. The theater masks — how many, what style? Specific designs, or should we propose options?

21. The "Stories are waiting" paper on the desk — should it be handwritten (Nali's handwriting) or typed?

22. What's on the walls besides the deer head? The screenplay mentions daggers, swords, alien-like sculptures — do you have these, or do we need to find them?

23. The locked room door — should it look different from the other door? Should there be any visual hint that something is behind it?

---

## Section 6: Logistics & Budget

24. When do you want to shoot? Do you have a target date?

25. How many shooting days do you estimate for this one scene?

26. What crew do you already have committed? (DP, sound, gaffer, etc.)

27. What equipment is available? (Camera body, lenses, lights, dolly/track, steadicam?)

28. Is there a budget ceiling for this test shoot that we should know about?

29. Do you need us to prepare a call sheet, or does your AD handle that?

---

## Section 7: What Comes Next?

30. If the test shoot goes well, what's the timeline for full production?

31. Do you want us to prepare preproduction packages for other scenes too, or just Scene 12 for now?

32. Is there anything else on your mind about this scene that we haven't asked about?

---

**We're ready — whatever you need, we're here.**
