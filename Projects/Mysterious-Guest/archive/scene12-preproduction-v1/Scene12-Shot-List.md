## Shot List by Shooting Order

Shots are grouped by camera setup to minimize equipment moves. Within each group, shoot widest framing first, then tighten — this protects lighting continuity and lets actors build performance.

### GROUP 10 — Setup L: The Locked Door

**Priority: HIGH — Critical story moment. Schedule when actress is fresh for performance.**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Shot | 12.14 |
| Setup | L |
| Shot Style | Wide Shot |
| Frame Size | Wide — full body Gesha, Nali visible in background |
| Lens | 28mm prime |
| Camera Height | Eye level |
| Camera Movement | Static or very slow track (0.3m over 20 seconds — barely perceptible) |
| Support | Tripod (or dolly for micro-move) |
| Characters | Gesha (foreground, at doors), Nali (background, living room area) |
| Dialogue | Yes — "This room?" / "That one's closed. The other one." |
| Sound | Boom + both lavs. Capture the SOUND of the locked door handle — the resistance, the metal. This is a sound design moment. |
| Lighting | Door area slightly darker than main room — she is stepping into shadow. Nali in background lit warmly. The contrast creates a visual boundary she is crossing. |
| Duration | 20 seconds |
| Estimated Takes | 4–6 (performance-dependent) |
| Estimated Time | 20 min |
| Director Notes | THE SEED SHOT. This locked door pays off 17 scenes later when Gesha's body is hidden and later discovered. But right now, it should feel like ALMOST NOTHING. A passing moment. The audience should barely register it consciously — but their subconscious files it away. Gesha tries the handle. It doesn't open. She looks back at Nali. THE LOOK is the shot. She wants to ask "why is this locked?" but she swallows it. Why? Because she's a guest. Because she doesn't want to seem suspicious. Because part of her doesn't want to know. Nali's response — "That one's closed" — should be delivered with ZERO tension. As if he said "the milk's in the fridge." The wide framing lets the architecture speak: two doors, one open, one closed. One path available, one denied. |

| Duration | 12 seconds |
| Estimated Takes | 2–3 |
| Estimated Time | 10 min |
| Director Notes | FINAL IMAGE OF THE SCENE. After Nali exits frame, HOLD THE EMPTY ROOM for 3–4 seconds before cutting. The house watches. The deer head stares at the empty space where people were. The masks hang in shadow. This is the room that will witness everything that follows in this film. Let it breathe. Nali's actions: stands still for a beat, picks up phone (checks it, puts it in pocket), grabs a piece of meat from the kitchen counter, walks toward the entrance door, exits frame RIGHT. His face: the charm is gone. This is his private face. Not sinister — just alone. Tired. A man who performs for everyone, finally offstage. |
