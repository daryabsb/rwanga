## Contingency Notes

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Tracking shot (12.1) takes too long | Cap at 8 takes. If no clean take by take 8, consider splitting into two shots (entrance wide + Gesha reaction) and combining in edit. |
| Actor fatigue on dialogue shots | Schedule intensive performance shots (Blocks 2, 10) with rest blocks between them (Blocks 6, 9 have no actors). |
| Equipment failure (dolly/slider) | Shots 12.1 and 12.5 can be done handheld as fallback. 12.11 tracking can become static. Quality changes but coverage is preserved. |
| Lighting consistency across blocks | Photograph the lighting setup after Block 1 for reference. All practicals should be on dimmers — label each dimmer position. |
| Time overrun | Block 9 (room reveal, 12.2) is the sacrificial shot — it can be cut or reduced to 1 take if time is tight. It's beautiful but not story-critical. |
| Props not ready | Deer head and masks are critical — everything else can be improvised. Have backup plan for deer head (large framed photograph of a deer as last resort). |
