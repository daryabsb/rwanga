## Location Summary

The garden house interior is the single location for Scene 12 and the primary location for approximately 60% of the film (Scenes 3, 12–29, 32, 42). Getting this location right is not just about one test shoot — it defines the visual identity of the entire film.

Based on the reference photos of the proposed location, the space is promising: a long rectangular room (~4m × 8m) with a dark ceiling, layered Persian rugs, wooden shelving, pendant lamps, and an eclectic artistic atmosphere. It already feels like a theater artist's living space. The work needed is refinement and set dressing, not transformation.

## SECTION 1: Space & Architecture Requirements

### Room Dimensions

| Requirement | Minimum | Ideal | Current Location |
|-------------|---------|-------|-----------------|
| Length | 6m | 8m+ | ~8m (confirmed from photos) |
| Width | 3.5m | 4m+ | ~4m (estimated) |
| Ceiling Height | 2.4m | 2.7m+ | ~2.5m (estimated — low, dark ceiling visible) |
| Floor to Door Clearance | Standard | Standard | Confirmed — entrance door visible |

**Assessment:** The room is long enough for the tracking shot (12.1 needs ~3m of dolly travel from entrance). Width is tight but workable — camera crew will need to be compact for side-angle setups (D, I). The low dark ceiling is actually an asset — it adds to the oppressive, cave-like feeling the scene needs.

### Doors

| Requirement | Detail | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Entrance Door | Main entry from outside/porch. Must open inward for tracking shot. Wide enough for two people and camera. | Present — visible in reference photos |
| Door 1 (LOCKED) | Must be lockable from outside (handle turns but door doesn't open). Must look solid. Should feel like it hides something. | Dark blue/black door visible in reference photo — back-right wall. Confirm it can be locked for the scene. |
| Door 2 (Nali's Room) | Must open easily. Gesha enters this room at scene end. Should be visible alongside Door 1 so both are in frame for Shot 12.14. | Door beside the locked door — confirm from reference. Test that it opens smoothly and silently. |
| Door Spacing | Both doors should be close enough to appear in a single wide shot (28mm lens, ~2m from camera). | Appears achievable from reference — both doors are on the same wall section. |

**Critical Action:** Visit location and test both doors. The locked door needs a functioning lock — the audience must hear and see Gesha try the handle and feel the resistance. Oil the hinges on Nali's room door — it must open silently so the sound design moment belongs entirely to the locked door.

## SECTION 2: Power & Electrical

| Requirement | Detail | Action |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| Power Access | Minimum 2 independent circuits for lighting. One for practical set lights, one for film lighting (LED panels). | Identify circuit breaker. Map which outlets are on which circuit. Label them. |
| Outlet Locations | Need outlets on at least 2 walls (east and west) for positioning LED panels. | Verify. Bring 3× 10m extension cables as backup. |
| Practical Lights | Pendant lamps (2–3) must be on dimmers. Existing lamps use standard bulbs? LED? | Visit location. Replace any daylight-temp bulbs with warm 2700K–3000K. Bring compatible dimmers (check bulb type — LED dimmers differ from incandescent). |
| Total Power Draw | Estimated: 500–800W (LED panels ~150W each, practicals ~200W total, misc ~100W). | Should be within standard residential capacity. No generator needed unless location has weak wiring. |
| Backup | Bring 1× portable power bank or small generator as failsafe for critical lights (deer head glint light, mask dim practical). | Especially important if location is rural/remote with unstable power. |

## SECTION 3: Sound Environment

| Requirement | Detail | Action |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| Ambient Noise | Interior must be quiet enough for dialogue recording. No persistent mechanical noise (HVAC, refrigerator hum, generator). | Visit location and LISTEN for 5 minutes in silence. Record a room tone sample on phone. |
| Refrigerator | If present in kitchen area — unplug during takes. Assign someone to remember to replug between setups. | Identify and label with "UNPLUG DURING TAKES" sign. |
| Traffic / Road Noise | Exterior noise must not bleed through walls/windows during dialogue shots (7 shots have dialogue). | Close all windows. Hang sound blankets over the curtained window if needed. Schedule dialogue-heavy blocks away from any known noise patterns (prayer calls, school hours, traffic peaks). |
| Flooring Sound | Footsteps are a sound design element (especially Shot 12.1 entrance and 12.14 walking to doors). Rug footsteps = soft, tile footsteps = sharp. Know the difference. | Map which areas are rugged vs. bare tile. Use this in blocking — Gesha on rug = intimate, Gesha on tile near doors = exposed. |
| Room Reverb | The room's natural reverb character affects dialogue quality. Low ceilings + rugs + fabric = warm, dry sound (good). | Record a clap test on location visit. If reverb is too live, add sound blankets out of frame. |
| Exterior Sound (Post) | The script implies the garden is quiet at evening. Occasional bird, wind, or distant dog bark can be added in post. | Capture 2–3 minutes of clean exterior ambience on location visit for post-production options. |

## SECTION 4: Lighting Conditions

| Requirement | Detail | Action |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| Natural Light | Evening setting — the curtains should show fading daylight through gaps. In reality, shoot time may be daytime. | Control natural light with blackout curtains or additional heavy drapes BEHIND the existing curtains. Then fake the evening gap-light with a gelled LED outside the window (CTO full + 1/2 — warm amber). |
| Window | One window on back wall behind curtains. Others? Check all walls. | Unwanted windows must be blacked out. Even small light leaks will read on camera with the 18mm wide lens (Shot 12.2). |
| Existing Practicals | Pendant lamps are the primary light source in the scene. They define the amber palette. | Replace bulbs to match: all warm (2700K), all same intensity. Mismatched pendants look accidental, not designed. |
| Darkness | The scene needs DARK areas — the ceiling, the corners, the mask area. Do not over-light. | Resist the instinct to fill every shadow. The darkness is a character. Brief the gaffer: "If you can see into every corner, it's too bright." |
| Color Temperature | The entire scene lives in warm amber (2700K–3200K). No cool light sources unless the faked window light is slightly cooler (4000K) to contrast. | Check ALL existing light sources: LED strips on ceiling (visible in photos — what color temp?). Remove or gel any cool-white sources. |

### Existing Light Sources Audit (from photos)

| Source | Location | Status | Action Needed |
|--------|----------|--------|---------------|
| LED strip light | Back wall, above curtains (horizontal) | Cool white (~5000K) | PROBLEM — this will kill the warm palette. Options: 1) Turn off entirely. 2) Gel with full CTO. 3) Replace with warm LED strip. |
| Pendant lamp 1 | Center, above dining table | Warm tone | Good — verify bulb temp, put on dimmer |
| Pendant lamp 2 | Above kitchen/shelf area | Warm tone | Good — verify and dim |
| Pendant lamp 3 | Near back wall | Warm/mixed | Verify — match to lamp 1 |
| Shelf lighting | East wall shelving | Warm (candle-like from photos) | Good — these create the golden shelf glow. Leave as-is or dim slightly. |
| Track lights | Ceiling (visible in photo 3) | Unknown color temp | CHECK. If cool, turn off or gel. |

**Critical Issue:** The LED strip light on the back wall (visible in photo 2) appears to be cool white. It must be addressed — it will clash with the warm amber palette and create an ugly mixed-temperature look on the deer head and curtains. Turning it off is the simplest fix if other lighting covers the area.

## SECTION 5: Access & Logistics

| Requirement | Detail | Action |
