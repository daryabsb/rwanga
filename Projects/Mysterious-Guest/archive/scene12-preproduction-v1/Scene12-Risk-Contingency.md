# RISK & CONTINGENCY NOTES — Scene 12

**Film:** Mysterious Guest (میوانێکی نادیار)
**Director:** Sarwar Muhedin
**Scene:** 12 — INT. Garden House — Evening
**Status:** LOCKED v1.0

---

## Risk Assessment Overview

Scene 12 is an interior shoot with controlled conditions, which lowers the overall risk profile compared to exteriors. The primary risks are: time overrun on complex setups, prop sourcing failures, equipment malfunction, and actor performance under pressure. Each risk is rated, and a specific contingency is provided.

---

## CATEGORY 1: Time & Schedule Risks

### Risk 1.1 — Tracking Shot (12.1) Eats the Schedule

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | HIGH |
| Impact | HIGH |
| Description | Shot 12.1 is a complex single take: dolly tracking 3m, pan, hold, actor blocking with precise timing. It's the most technically demanding shot in the scene. Each take cycle is ~4 min (performance + reset + notes). 8 takes = 32 min of rolling alone, plus rehearsal and adjustments. |
| Contingency | **Hard cap at take 8.** If no clean take by take 8, split the shot into two: (A) Wide static of both entering, cut to (B) Medium on Gesha's reaction. Coverage is preserved, the single-take elegance is lost but the scene still works. The DP should be briefed on this split in advance so they can set up quickly if called. |
| Prevention | Extensive blocking rehearsal during pre-call (15 min minimum). Walk the dolly path 3 times without rolling. Mark focus points, pan stop, and Gesha's stop position with tape. The more rehearsed, the fewer takes. |

### Risk 1.2 — Dialogue Shots Run Long

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | MEDIUM |
| Impact | MEDIUM |
| Description | 7 shots have dialogue. Dialogue takes generally require more takes than silent shots — line flubs, overlapping deliveries, emotional resets. The director may want additional takes to explore performance variations. |
| Contingency | **The sacrificial shot is 12.2 (room reveal).** It's a beautiful establishing shot with no actors and no dialogue — it can be cut to 1 take or dropped entirely without losing any story content. This frees 10 minutes. Additionally, Blocks 6 and 9 (no actors) can be trimmed if needed. |
| Prevention | Print dialogue sides for actors the day before. Run lines off-set during Blocks 6 and 9 (no-actor blocks) to prepare for upcoming dialogue blocks. |

### Risk 1.3 — Director Wants More Coverage

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | MEDIUM |
| Impact | MEDIUM–HIGH |
| Description | The director may want additional angles or shots not in the locked breakdown — an extra insert, a reverse angle, a wider version of a medium. This is normal and creative, but on a 5-hour day it can push past wrap time. |
| Contingency | AD tracks time publicly — announce "We are on schedule" or "We are 15 minutes over" at each break. If the director requests additional shots, the AD presents the trade: "We can add this, but we lose Block 9 (room reveal) or we lose the wild sound recording at the end." The director decides. Never say no — present choices. |
| Prevention | Review the shot list with the director the day before the shoot. Confirm: "These 15 shots are what we're shooting. Anything you want to add or change?" Settle it before the day, not during. |

## CATEGORY 2: Equipment Risks

### Risk 2.1 — Dolly/Track Failure

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW |
| Impact | HIGH (affects Shots 12.1, 12.5, 12.11) |
| Description | Dolly wheel jam, track misalignment, uneven floor causing bumpy movement. |
| Contingency | **Steadicam fallback** for 12.1 (if operator is available). **Handheld** for 12.1 (changes the aesthetic but preserves the single-take entrance). **Slider fallback** for 12.5 and 12.11 — both shorter moves that a slider can handle. |
| Prevention | Test dolly on location floor the day before. Bring shims for leveling. Confirm wheels are clean and bearings are smooth. Bring a backup slider regardless. |

### Risk 2.2 — Camera or Lens Failure

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW |
| Impact | CRITICAL |
| Description | Sensor malfunction, card failure, lens element damage. |
| Contingency | **Backup camera body** (even a lower-spec body is better than nothing — a DSLR/mirrorless as last resort). The most-used lens is the 50mm (6 shots) — if it fails, the 40mm can substitute for most shots. The 85mm (4 shots) has no close substitute — protect it. |
| Prevention | Camera and lenses checked + cleaned the day before. Cards formatted and tested. Bring minimum 2 cards and rotate (never shoot an entire day on one card). Lens caps on when not rolling. |

### Risk 2.3 — Lighting Equipment Failure

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW–MEDIUM |
| Impact | MEDIUM |
| Description | LED panel failure, dimmer malfunction, practical bulb blowout. |
| Contingency | Bring **spare bulbs** (warm 2700K, same socket type as practicals). If an LED panel fails, rearrange the remaining panel to cover the most critical function (Key Fill > Window Fake). The dedolight for the deer head eye-glint is replaceable with a **phone flashlight** or **small torch** with CTO gel as an ugly-but-functional emergency fix. |
| Prevention | Power all lighting on the day before to confirm function. Bring backup dimmers. Label every cable and power connection. |

### Risk 2.4 — Sound Equipment Failure

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW |
| Impact | HIGH (7 dialogue shots) |
| Description | Wireless lav interference, boom cable failure, recorder malfunction. |
| Contingency | **Camera scratch audio** becomes the backup dialogue source. Ensure camera-mounted mic (even a built-in) is running as the absolute last resort. If one lav fails, the remaining lav + boom is sufficient — most professional productions work with boom-primary anyway. If the boom fails, both lavs cover the dialogue. If the recorder fails: **stop shooting dialogue until fixed.** There is no fallback for zero audio. |
| Prevention | Fresh batteries in all wireless transmitters at call time. Backup batteries on set. Frequency scan for RF interference at the location during scout. Test all cables the day before. |

## CATEGORY 3: Prop & Set Risks

### Risk 3.1 — Deer Head Not Available

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | MEDIUM (depends on local sourcing) |
| Impact | HIGH (appears in 6 shots, referenced in dialogue) |
| Description | A realistic taxidermy deer head may be difficult to source in the Sulaymaniyah area on short notice. Rental, purchase, or borrow may all fall through. |
| Contingency | **Tier 1:** High-quality replica from theatrical/online supplier (order 2+ weeks early). **Tier 2:** A large, realistic PAINTING or PHOTOGRAPH of a deer in a heavy ornate frame, mounted in the same position. The dialogue can be adjusted: "Look at those eyes in that painting" instead of referencing a real head. The visual impact is reduced but the scene still functions. **Tier 3:** A different animal head (boar, ram, mountain goat) if one is locally available. The screenplay specifies deer but the story beat works with any imposing mounted animal. **Confirm with director which fallback is acceptable.** |
| Prevention | Start sourcing IMMEDIATELY — this is the longest lead-time prop. Contact hunters' associations, antique dealers, theater prop houses, and taxidermists in the Sulaymaniyah/Erbil area. Post on local buy/sell groups if needed. |

### Risk 3.2 — Deer Head Falls During Shooting

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW (if properly mounted) |
| Impact | CRITICAL (safety hazard + prop damage + lost time) |
| Description | A taxidermy head is heavy (5–10 kg). If wall mounting fails, it could fall and injure crew or damage the prop beyond use. |
| Contingency | **Stop shooting.** Check for injuries. Assess prop damage. If repairable, remount with stronger hardware. If destroyed, switch to Tier 2 fallback (painting/photo). |
| Prevention | **Test mount under load the day before.** Use appropriate wall anchors (not just nails — use toggle bolts or masonry anchors depending on wall material). Add a safety wire (thin steel cable from the back of the mount to a second anchor point above). The wire holds the head if the primary mount fails. This is standard practice for overhead set dressing. |

### Risk 3.3 — Locked Door Doesn't Lock Convincingly

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | MEDIUM |
| Impact | MEDIUM (Shot 12.14 depends on the door feeling genuinely locked) |
| Description | The door may not have a functioning lock, or the handle may not communicate "locked" convincingly on camera or in sound. |
| Contingency | **Install a simple deadbolt or sliding bolt** on the door before the shoot. Even a surface-mounted bolt (visible from Gesha's side) adds visual information: this door is deliberately secured. If the handle itself feels too loose, add a **rubber door stop** wedged underneath from the far side — the door genuinely won't open when pushed. |
| Prevention | Test the door on the scout day. Confirm: does the handle rattle when turned? Does the door give slightly then stop? Does it SOUND locked? If any of these fail, install hardware the day before. |

## CATEGORY 4: Actor & Performance Risks

### Risk 4.1 — Actors Not Cast in Time

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | MEDIUM (test shoot may have compressed timeline) |
| Impact | CRITICAL (no actors = no shoot) |
| Description | If the director hasn't finalized casting for Nali and Gesha, the entire shoot is delayed. |
| Contingency | Set a **casting deadline** of 1 week before shoot date. If the deadline passes without confirmed cast, postpone the shoot date. It is better to delay 1 week than to shoot with unprepared or wrong actors — the test shoot is meant to validate the vision, and miscasting defeats the purpose. |
| Prevention | Include casting status in every production meeting. Offer to help: provide character breakdowns, organize audition space, review self-tapes. |

### Risk 4.2 — Actor Fatigue / Emotional Flatness

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | MEDIUM |
| Impact | MEDIUM–HIGH |
| Description | 5 hours of shooting one scene. The dialogue requires emotional range — curiosity, fear, charm, guardedness. By Block 10 (locked door), the actress playing Gesha may be emotionally drained, and this is the scene's most important performance moment. |
| Contingency | The schedule already places actor-free blocks (6, 9) as rest periods. If fatigue is evident before Block 10, **call an unscheduled 10-minute break** and have the director speak to the actress privately — not about the scene, about anything else. A mental reset. If performance is still flat after the break, consider shooting Block 10 as a **series of shorter takes** rather than full-length passes — the editor can assemble the performance from pieces. |
| Prevention | Schedule design already accounts for this (intensive blocks separated by rest blocks). Ensure craft services has protein and sugar available — low blood sugar kills performance. The director should speak to both actors at the top of the day: "We have 15 shots. Not all of them are equally important. I will ask for what I need — trust me to manage your energy." |

### Risk 4.3 — Actor Injury on Set

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW |
| Impact | HIGH |
| Description | Trip on rug edges, bump into furniture in dim lighting, cut on prop daggers (if real). |
| Contingency | **First aid kit on set** (bandages, antiseptic, ice pack, pain relief). For minor injury: treat and continue if actor is willing. For significant injury: **wrap the day.** The footage captured so far is not lost. Remaining shots can be picked up on a second day. Never pressure an injured actor to continue. |
| Prevention | Tape down rug edges. Mark dolly track and cable runs with glow tape for visibility. If real bladed props are on walls, position them OUT OF REACH (high enough that no one casually touches them). Brief all crew on prop weapon locations. Ensure walking paths are clear and well-lit during setup/teardown (when the "mood lighting" is not appropriate for safety). |

## CATEGORY 5: Location & Environment Risks

### Risk 5.1 — Power Outage

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW–MEDIUM (depends on area reliability) |
| Impact | HIGH (all lighting + camera + sound depends on power) |
| Description | If the location is in a rural area outside Sulaymaniyah, power stability may be unreliable. |
| Contingency | Bring a **portable generator** or large battery station (e.g., EcoFlow, Jackery 1000W+) as backup for critical equipment (camera, recorder, monitor). LED panels and practicals can run on battery for limited time. If power drops during a take: hold positions, don't call cut — the take may be usable up to the dropout point. |
| Prevention | Ask the property owner about power reliability. If outages are common, schedule the shoot during the most stable hours (often mid-morning to early afternoon, not peak evening). Consider renting a silent generator as insurance. |

### Risk 5.2 — Unexpected Exterior Noise

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | MEDIUM (rural location = unpredictable) |
| Impact | MEDIUM (dialogue shots affected) |
| Description | Construction, agricultural machinery, aircraft, neighbor's music, animals, prayer calls. Any of these can ruin a dialogue take. |
| Contingency | **Hold for sound.** When the mixer hears noise incoming, they call "HOLD" before the take, or "SOUND NOTE" during. The AD decides: wait for the noise to pass, or proceed and plan for ADR (automated dialogue replacement in post). For prayer calls: they are predictable (5 times daily, check local schedule). Plan non-dialogue blocks during the nearest call time. |
| Prevention | Visit the location at the SAME TIME OF DAY you plan to shoot. Listen for 15 minutes. Note patterns. Ask the property owner: "Is there construction nearby? Do neighbors have loud dogs? Is there a mosque within hearing range?" |

### Risk 5.3 — Location Access Denied Day-Of

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW (if properly arranged) |
| Impact | CRITICAL (no location = no shoot) |
| Description | Property owner changes mind, gate locked, access road blocked, miscommunication on dates. |
| Contingency | **There is no same-day fix for this.** The shoot is postponed. All crew and cast are informed immediately and sent home. The production manager contacts the property owner to resolve and reschedule. |
| Prevention | **Written agreement** with the property owner: dates, hours, crew size, what will be done to the property, compensation (if any). Confirm by phone 48 hours before AND the evening before. Have the property owner's direct phone number. Have a backup contact (neighbor, family member) in case the owner is unreachable. |

## CATEGORY 6: Data & Footage Risks

### Risk 6.1 — Card / Data Loss

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW |
| Impact | CATASTROPHIC (lost footage = lost shoot day) |
| Description | Camera card corruption, accidental deletion, card physical damage. |
| Contingency | **Dual card recording** if the camera supports it (mirror to both slots). If not: swap cards at each break and immediately offload the completed card to a laptop. A card that's in a bag is safer than a card that's in the camera for 5 hours straight. |
| Prevention | **Never format a card until it's been backed up to two separate drives.** Use cards from reputable brands (SanDisk Extreme Pro, Sony Tough). Never remove a card while the camera is still writing. Label each card with a number and log which blocks are on which card. |

### Risk 6.2 — Audio / Video Sync Issues

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Likelihood | LOW–MEDIUM |
| Impact | MEDIUM (fixable in post but costs time) |
| Description | If timecode is not synced between camera and recorder, audio and video will need manual sync in post. Clapper/slate sync can fail if the clap is out of frame or inaudible. |
| Contingency | If the clapper is missed on a take, have the actor CLAP HANDS visibly on camera at the start or end of the take — this creates a sync point. In post, use waveform matching software (PluralEyes, DaVinci Resolve auto-sync) as a backup. |
| Prevention | **Jam timecode** between camera and recorder at the start of the day. Re-jam at each break. If no timecode capability: use the slate for every take without exception. 2nd AC (or PA) is responsible for holding the slate in frame and snapping it clearly. The boom mic must hear the clap. |
